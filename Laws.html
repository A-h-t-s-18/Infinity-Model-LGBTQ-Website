Explore the timeline, major rulings, legal protections, myths vs facts, and test your knowledge with a dynamic quiz.

Legal Timeline

1860: Section 377 introduced under British rule.
2009: Delhi High Court decriminalizes homosexuality.
2013: Supreme Court restores Section 377.
2014: NALSA recognizes transgender rights.
2018: Supreme Court reads down Section 377.
2019: Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act.

Major Laws & Judgements

Myth vs Fact

Myth: “Transgender people need surgery to be recognized.”

Fact: Self-identification is valid (NALSA, 2014).
Myth: “LGBTQ+ rights are not in the Indian Constitution.”

Fact: Articles 14, 15, 19 & 21 protect gender & sexuality rights.
